# Teacher Patterns – Ecocom Français (chap. 1–2)

This file describes typical patterns of an Ecocom French teacher working with chapitres 1–2 (telephone, dates, email, forms).

---

## 1. General Habits

- Follows **Ecocom** book structure closely.  
- Reuses **phrases and models** from the book almost word-for-word.  
- Values **clear structure** more than creativity.  
- Tests are **short but dense**, with many small tasks (points) instead of one big text.

---

## 2. Typical Exam Components

In a standard test for chap. 1–2, expect:

1. **Dictation of numbers / codes**  
   - Teacher reads numbers and sometimes letters twice.  
   - Student writes digits or codes correctly.  
   - No AI help during the dictation.

2. **Info note / fiche d’inscription**  
   - Student fills in a form using dictated / given information.  
   - Often includes: name, first name, date, time, phone number, e-mail.

3. **Telephone situations**  
   - Short situations in German: “Sie arbeiten am Empfang …”  
   - Student writes **one or two French sentences** representing what the receptionist says.  
   - Teacher expects **standard Ecocom phrases** (not creative paraphrases).

4. **Dialogue with gaps**  
   - Pre-printed phone dialogue with missing words.  
   - Missing words are usually: *appareil, voyage d’affaires, rappeler, message, lentement, plus fort*, etc.

5. **Short professional email**  
   - Student writes **one email** with correct structure and polite formulas.  
   - Topic: often a **request for information**, **confirmation**, or **inscription**.

---

## 3. Marking & What Is Rewarded

Teacher tends to reward:

- Correct **polite forms**:  
  - *Madame, Monsieur,*  
  - *Pourriez-vous…*  
  - *Veuillez…*  
  - *Meilleures salutations,* / *Cordiales salutations,*  

- Correct **place + date line**:  
  - *« Lausanne, le 10 décembre 2025 »*

- Correct use of **telephone formulas**:  
  - *« Ne quittez pas, je vous passe Monsieur… »*  
  - *« Il est en réunion. »*  
  - *« Je ne vous ai pas bien compris(e). »*

- Correct **spelling of numbers & codes**:  
  - right order, right spacing, correct forms when needed.

---

## 4. Typical Traps

1. **German structures translated too literally**

   - Example: *« Ich rufe wegen… »* → should be *« J’appelle au sujet de… »* not *« J’appelle pour la raison de… »*.

2. **Wrong or missing accents**

   - *Pouriez vous* instead of *Pourriez-vous*  
   - *Meilleurs salutations* instead of *Meilleures salutations*

3. **Wrong date format**

   - German style: `3. Juni 2025` → in French wrongly as *« 3. Juin 2025 »*.  
   - Correct: *« le 3 juin 2025 »* (with “le” and lowercase month).

4. **Email without structure**

   - No subject line.  
   - No polite form of address.  
   - No final greetings.

5. **Overly complex or “too perfect” style**

   - Very advanced vocabulary or long, elegant sentences can look suspicious for this level.  
   - Safer to stay close to the Ecocom models.

---

## 5. Strategy for Safe Answers

To align with teacher expectations:

- Use **simple, standard Ecocom phrases** from the other files.  
- For each telephone or email task, pick **one clear model** and adapt only small elements (names, dates, times).  
- Make sure the **key vocabulary** from the book appears in the answer:  
  - telephone: *appareil, réunion, voyage d’affaires, rappeler, message*  
  - email: *Objet, Madame, Monsieur, Pourriez-vous, Veuillez, Meilleures salutations*.
